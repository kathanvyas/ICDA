Cohort Composition & Demographics

Before identifying predictors, we established a demographic baseline. Features such as Birth Weight, Gestational Age, and Gender were analyzed for correlation. While Gender distribution shows a male majority, statistical tests (p-values) confirm these demographics are not independent predictors of Sudden Hypoxemia.

◉ Patient Gender Distribution

The cohort is composed of 61.1% male and 38.9% female patients. Statistical analysis (p=0.15) suggests gender is not a significant predictor of SH.

◉ Prematurity & Risk

Only 17.2% of the cohort was categorized as premature. No significant correlation was found between prematurity and SH events (p=0.76).

Critical Temporal Markers

The timing of surgical intervention proved to be a pivotal factor. Patients who suffered Sudden Hypoxemia underwent surgery at a significantly lower median age (7.3 days) compared to the stable cohort (9.0 days), marking Age at Surgery as a high-leverage predictive variable.

◉ Median Age at Surgery Comparison

Takeaway: The P-value of 0.049 highlights that earlier surgical timing is a statistically relevant predictor for future Sudden Hypoxemia events.

Feature Significance Ranking

To separate signal from noise, features were ranked by their p-values. Complications like Arrhythmia and LCOS emerged with the highest statistical significance (p=0.0001), while weight and age markers showed lower predictive power.

High Significance Drivers

Arrhythmia and LCOS are leading indicators. These complications are often precursors to SH, requiring proactive monitoring once diagnosed.

Low Significance Factors

Birth Weight and Gestational Age, while important for general care, do not statistically differentiate SH risk in this N=157 sample size.

Temporal Complication Correlation

A deep dive into complication timing reveals that for significant features like LCOS and Arrhythmia, the diagnosis frequently occurs BEFORE the Sudden Hypoxemia event. This window of opportunity is critical for clinical decision support.

◉ LCOS Diagnosis Timing

80% of LCOS cases were diagnosed BEFORE the SH event, providing a potential warning signal for clinical teams.

◉ Arrhythmia Diagnosis Timing

50% of Arrhythmia cases in SH patients were documented before the hypoxemia threshold was reached.

NICU Predictive Analytics Summary

This data suggests that earlier surgical age and the onset of clinical complications (Arrhythmia/LCOS) are the strongest predictors of Sudden Hypoxemia. Future predictive models should focus on temporal vital patterns following these diagnoses.
