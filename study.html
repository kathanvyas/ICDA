Comprehensive Overview: UI/UX & AI Evaluation

Guiding medical students in conducting rigorous studies for complex NICU dashboards.

Context: You are evaluating a next-generation medical dashboard for Single Ventricle Disease (SVD) neonates. This study evaluates both standard data visualization heuristics AND advanced artificial intelligence integration.

📊 Baseline UX Elements:
  • Real-time visualization of vitals (SpO2, HR, BP).
  • Lactate trend lines and tabular data presentation.
  • General layout, visual hierarchy, and cognitive load.
  • Goal: Measure Time-to-Recognition and usability (SUS).
🤖 AI/CDSS Elements:
  • Predictive ML: 1-hour Shunt Occlusion Risk Score (%).
  • Explainable AI (XAI): "Top Features" driving the risk score.
  • Generative LLM: Automated text summaries and "Next Steps".
  • Goal: Measure clinician trust and automation bias.

Combined Study Protocol

Methodology for executing clinical user studies involving both standard UI and predictive models.

Phase 1: Preparation & Setup

  • 📝
    Define Core Scenarios: Create realistic vignettes of SVD patients. Include scenarios with subtle shunt occlusions (dropping SpO2, rising lactate) to test baseline UI Time-to-Recognition.
  • 🧪
    Define AI Scenarios: Design a "High Trust" scenario (AI is correct and explains well) and an "Edge Case/Conflict" scenario (AI gives a high score due to artifact data). Goal: Will they catch the AI's mistake via the XAI?
  • 💻
    Prototype Validation: Pre-load all data. For LLM text outputs, "hardcode" the summaries so every user sees the exact same generative text, ensuring a controlled variable environment.
  • 👥
    Participant Recruitment: Recruit 12-15 participants (NICU nurses, neonatologists, cardiology fellows). Prepare informed consent without revealing the exact clinical event or AI focus.

Phase 2: Execution (The Session)

  • 🎙️
    Think-Aloud Protocol: Instruct the participant to verbalize thought processes. "Tell me what you are looking at, what it means to you, and what you would do next."
  • ⏱️
    Standard UI Tracking: Use screen recording to note the exact time it takes them to manually identify the shunt occlusion indicators before the AI intervenes.
  • 🤖
    AI Probing: When they view the 1-hour risk score or LLM 'Next Steps', pause and probe: "Do you agree with this percentage? Would you execute this LLM order without further checks?"
  • 🔄
    Counterbalancing: Randomize the order of variants presented (e.g., UI only vs. UI + AI) to prevent learning bias. Administer SUS and NASA-TLX post-scenario.

Phase 3: Analysis of UX & Trust

  • 📈
    Quantitative Metrics: Calculate Time-to-Recognition. Run t-tests. Compare standard SUS and TLX scores across different dashboard variants.
  • ⚖️
    Measure Automation Bias: In the "Edge Case" scenario, calculate the percentage of clinicians who agreed with the incorrect AI Risk Score. This quantifies dangerous over-reliance.
  • 🧩
    XAI Comprehension: Code transcripts to see if the "Top Features" visualizations aided physiological understanding or caused confusion.
  • 📊
    Trust Scoring: Supplement standard analysis with Trust in Automated Systems Survey (TASS) data.

Comprehensive Precautions

Mitigating standard clinical testing biases alongside AI-specific risks.

⚕️ Clinician Fatigue

Keep sessions under 45 mins. Accommodate interruptions. Do not schedule during shift handoffs.

🧠 Minimizing Bias

Do not lead the participant. Reply neutrally: "What does the dashboard tell you?"

🔒 Data Privacy (PHI)

Ensure absolutely ZERO real Protected Health Information is in the mockups. Use synthesized data.

📉 Ecological Validity

A quiet room isn't a NICU. Consider adding low-level background alarms to simulate cognitive load.

🤖 Automation Bias

Evaluate if the UI makes the AI look too authoritative, causing clinicians to ignore raw data.

👻 LLM Hallucinations

Test if users can identify plausible but clinically incorrect summaries generated by the LLM.

⚖️ Algorithmic Aversion

Monitor for outright dismissal of the AI Risk Score, especially if XAI explanations are too complex.

⚖️ Liability Implications

Ensure the UX clearly communicates LLM "Next Steps" are recommendations, not standing orders.
